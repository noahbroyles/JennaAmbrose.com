City of Refuge

    Three men ride into Hebron, a city of refuge during the time of David's flight from Saul. One has killed a man. It is up to Eliel the scribe, and his son, Phineas, to discover the details and whether the man is deserving the shelter of the city. Was it an accident? A crime of passion? Envy? Revenge? Or Love?

    Hello, and welcome to my website! Many people that know me know that I am not just a writer, but a reader as well. Here is a list of books that I really love. If you have a recommendation for me I would love to hear about it.

  • The Scottish Chiefs, Jane Porter (my all-time favorite)
  • The Pathfinder, The Last of the Mohicans, The Deerslayer, and The Spy, James Fenimore Cooper
  • Ivanhoe, Sir Walter Scott
  • The Count of Monte Cristo, and The Three Musketeers, Alexandre Dumas
  • Pride and Prejudice, Emma, and Mansfield Park, Jane Austen. I like her other works, too, but these three are my favorite.
  • Laddie, Jean Stratton Porter.
  • Ben-Hur, Lew Wallace
  • Up From Slavery, Booker T. Washington
  • The Boys in the Boat, Daniel James Brown
  • Regency Buck, and Simon the Coldheart, Georgette Heyer
  • The Time Travelers, The Time Thief, and The Time Quake, Linda Buckley-Archer
  • Jane Eyre, Charlotte Bronte
  • To Kill a Mockingbird, Harper Lee

Many of you know that I have 5 children, and I make them read a lot. Also, I love to read aloud to the little ones. Sometimes it can be difficult to find good books at the library. Here is a list of books that I have found to be good, starting with the wee little ones and progressing in age:

  • Nursery Rhymes (Mother Goose is an example)
  • Beatrix Potter Books
  • Aesop's Fables
  • Old Mother West Wind, Thornton Burgess
  • The Adventures of Chatterer, the Red Squirrel, Thornton Burgess
  • Winnie the Pooh, A.A. Milne
  • Berenstain Bears Books, Stan and Jan Berenstain
